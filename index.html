Avinash Aravind

Hello! My name is Avinash, and I am a senior undergraduate student at Cornell University studying Atmospheric Science. I am from New Jersey and have been fascinated by the weather for over a decade now, and that fascination has only grown as a college student.

My research interests are in tropical meteorology and specifically tropical cyclones, their structure and dynamics, and how they may be influenced by climate change. I am also interested in both numerical modeling and machine learning applications in meteorology; I am pursuing minors in Applied Mathematics, Data Science, and Mathematics alongside my major.

Outside of coursework, I am part of the CU GeoData engineering student project team where I help design and build hardware prototypes for Earth Science observation as the Tech subteam lead. I also spent the last two years as the Forecast Chair for CCAMS, our university chapter of the American Meteorological Society and de-facto weather club, where I got to lead discussions on both ongoing weather events and hot topics in the field as a whole.
